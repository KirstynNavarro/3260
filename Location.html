Boracay is a small, world-renowned Philippine island in Aklan province known for its powdery white sand, vibrant nightlife, and stunning sunsets, specifically along its 4-kilometer White Beach.

Best Time to Visit: The dry season (Amihan) from November to April/May is ideal, featuring calm waters on the White Beach side.
How to Get There: Travelers typically fly into Caticlan (MPH) Airport, take a short tricycle ride to Caticlan Jetty Port, and a 10-15 minute boat ride to the island.

Activities

  • Ariel's Point

    • Ariel's Point is rated the #1 "Best Time" in Boracay. With cliff dives from 3, 5, 6, 7, & 13 meter cliff diving, snorkling, and kayaking; its no wonder why Ariel's Point is so popular. Included is the famous "all you can eat" Bbq buffet. Ariel's Point is sure to make your vacation perfect.

  • Island Hopping

    • Boracay island hopping typically lasts 3–5 hours, starting from White Beach (Stations 1-3) to explore nearby spots like Crocodile Island for snorkeling, Crystal Cove, and Puka Beach. Tours usually cost ₱500–₱2,000, often including lunch and boat transportation, with best conditions from November to May.

  • Zipline Boracay

    • Zipline Boracay is sure to keep you busy while you're in Boracay. Located directly near Fairways & Bluewater Resort, you'll find it easily. Zipline for hours in Boracay for fun and adventure.

Sights to See

  • Keyhole Beach

    • The Boracay Keyhole, also known as Cujo’s Keyhole or Lapus-Lapus, is a naturally formed rock arch located on the northeastern side of Boracay Island within the Boracay Newcoast development, Philippines. This scenic, iconic attraction is popular for photography, specifically framing the turquoise sea, sunrise views, and for its unique, nature-carved shape

  • Diniwid Beach

    • A small, intimate beach right next to Station 1 with gorgeous sunset views, quiet surroundings, and great for snorkeling

  • Willy's Rock

    • An iconic, photogenic volcanic formation with a Virgin Mary shrine, located on White Beach
